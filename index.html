Dribbling wasn't allowed back then

In the original rules players could not bounce the ball. In 1901, the rule was enacted permitting players to bounce the ball one time.

James Naismith invented basketball

Naismith's secretary typed up the rules and tacked them on the bulletin board. A short time later, the gym class met, and the teams were chosen with three centers, three forwards, and three guards per side. Two of the centers met at mid-court, Naismith tossed the ball, and the game of “basket ball” was born.

The Games was way shorter back then - 5 min quarters

In NBA each quarter played for 12 minutes. As the sum of 4 quarters equals to 48 minutes. If the end of the game scores are leveled then additional OT over time will be given which will had 5 minutes, if again the scores are leveled again same process goes on till the one team leads another team in scoring.

Referees used watches

In higher levels of college and professional basketball, officials wear a timing device on the belt-line called PTS Precision Timing System The device is used by on-court officials to start and stop the game clock in a timely manner, rather than waiting for the scoreboard operator (timekeeper) to do so.
